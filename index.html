Our Story

Tibetan Youth Initiative began as a student-led nonprofit rooted in Tibetan community and culture. We support youth through education programs, cultural projects, and practical resources that help them succeed in school and maintain their identity.

"We are students, volunteers, and friends working together to support our communities. Real progress happens through steady effort, thoughtful teamwork, and shared care for the people we serve."

Founder & Lead Director Nyida Gyal

Policy Briefs & Reports

Real projects and reports from our team, shared with international partners and organizations that shape policy and help keep Tibetan voices heard.

New Technologies and Transitional Justice

Submitted to the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights

2025

This report looks at how new technology affects justice work, and why responsible use matters. It highlights the need for clear standards, honest reporting, and safeguards so tools support people without creating new harms.

Authors: Nyida Gyal

Youth-Led Educational Interventions for Displaced Youth

Submitted to the European Commission, Directorate-General for Employment, Social Affairs and Inclusion

2026

Focused on refugee and minority children, this report argues for fair access to school supplies and community-led programs. It urges support for student voices, better tools for learning, and stronger plans to close education gaps.

Authors: Nyida Gyal, Thomas Millward, Khadotso Gyal, Tyler Jeong

Rights of Persons Belonging to National or Ethnic, Religious and Linguistic Minorities

Submitted to the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights

2026

Inspired by Tibetan diaspora experiences, this report describes the daily barriers minority communities face. It calls for better access to identity documents, fair participation in public life, and support for community-led decision making.

Authors: Khadotso Gyal, Thomas Millward, Nyida Gyal

Impact of Mental Health Challenges on Young People's Human Rights

Submitted to the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights

2026

This report treats mental health as a fundamental part of young people's well-being. It describes how stress and trauma affect learning and community life, and urges better support systems, respectful services, and clear protections for youth.

Schools We Supported

These are the schools where we partner directly with educators and families to deliver supplies, support programs, and preserve Tibetan language and culture.

Tibetan Children's Village

Tibetan Children’s Villages (TCV) is a nonprofit organization established in 1960 in Dharamsala to care for and educate Tibetan refugee orphans and destitute children. Over time, TCV has grown into a large network of children’s villages, residential schools, and day schools across India, serving nearly 10,000 children and youth, and remains dedicated to providing a holistic education and preserving Tibetan culture. Its mission is to ensure that every child under its care receives sound education, develops a strong cultural identity, and becomes a self-reliant member of society.

Mewoen Tsuglag Petoen School

Mewoen Tsuglag Petoen School, founded in 2005 in Dharamsala, is a model school for Tibetan children in exile that combines modern education with traditional Tibetan language and values. Managed by the Sambhota Tibetan Schools Society, it teaches in Tibetan up to class III and introduces additional languages later, focusing on preserving Tibetan culture while providing quality education.

Yongling Creche & Kinder Garten

Yongling Crèche & Kindergarten, established in McLeod Ganj in 1984, serves to support working parents and meet the exile Tibetan government’s educational responsibilities for Tibetan children. The kindergarten is committed to providing a nurturing environment that encourages early childhood education. Currently, the school enrolls about 75 children, and also offers hostel facilities for some students to further support their learning and care.
