Mission Statement

Tibetan Youth Initiative is a nonprofit organization dedicated to empowering Tibetan youth through education, cultural preservation, and community-driven development. We operate with the belief that sustainable social progress emerges from equitable access to educational resources, reinforced cultural identity, and youth engagement in community leadership.

Our work bridges diaspora communities and local institutions, focusing particularly on Tibetan students in Dharamsala while extending our advocacy reach to international forums.

Core Values

  • Educational Excellence: Quality learning resources, mentorship, and academic opportunity
  • Cultural Continuity: Tibetan language, traditions, and heritage preservation
  • Community Partnership: Collaborative relationships with schools, families, and local organizations
  • Evidence-Based Advocacy: Research-informed policy recommendations in international forums
  • Equity and Inclusion: Resources directed to historically marginalized youth populations
  • Accountability and Impact: Transparent operations and measurable programmatic outcomes

Institutional History

TYI began when Tibetan students in Dharamsala lacked consistent access to basic educational supplies. A student-led supply drive evolved into a comprehensive nonprofit combining direct educational support with evidence-based policy advocacy.

Today, TYI distributes educational resources to partner schools, preserves Tibetan cultural programming, and submits substantive policy briefs to international organizations.
