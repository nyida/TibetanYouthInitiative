About Our Organization

Understanding our mission, values, and commitment to Tibetan youth empowerment.

Our Mission

Tibetan Youth Initiative is a nonprofit organization dedicated to empowering Tibetan youth through education, cultural preservation, and community-driven development. We operate with the belief that sustainable social progress emerges from equitable access to educational resources, reinforced cultural identity, and youth engagement in community leadership.

Our work bridges diaspora communities and local institutions, focusing particularly on Tibetan students in Dharamsala while extending our advocacy reach to international forums.

Core Values

Educational Excellence

Ensuring Tibetan youth have access to quality learning resources, mentorship, and opportunities to excel academically and personally.

Cultural Continuity

Preserving and strengthening Tibetan language, traditions, and heritage as essential components of youth identity and resilience.

Community Partnership

Building collaborative relationships with schools, families, and local organizations to create sustainable systems of support.

Evidence-Based Advocacy

Developing research-informed policy recommendations that amplify Tibetan voices in international human rights and education discussions.

Equity & Inclusion

Ensuring resources and opportunities reach all youth, with particular attention to historically marginalized and vulnerable populations.

Accountability & Impact

Measuring outcomes, maintaining transparent operations, and ensuring every contribution directly benefits the communities we serve.

Leadership

Her vision centers on empowering youth to become agents of positive change within their communities while maintaining strong connections to Tibetan cultural heritage.

How We Started

Tibetan Youth Initiative began with a simple observation: Tibetan students in Dharamsala and across the diaspora lacked consistent access to basic educational supplies. More broadly, young Tibetans often felt disconnected from policy conversations affecting their futures and communities.

What started as a student-led supply drive evolved into a comprehensive nonprofit organization combining direct educational support with evidence-based policy advocacy. We recognized that lasting impact requires both immediate assistance and systemic change—supporting students today while advocating for equitable policies tomorrow.

Today, TYI operates across multiple initiatives: distributing educational resources to partner schools, preserving Tibetan cultural programming, and submitting substantive policy briefs to international organizations. Our team of students, volunteers, and advisors shares an unwavering commitment to Tibetan youth empowerment.
