Policy Briefs & Research

Evidence-based submissions advancing international discourse on youth rights, education equity, and minority protections.

Our policy research team develops substantive, evidence-based briefs that contribute to international policy conversations. These submissions to UN bodies and regional organizations articulate the experiences and priorities of Tibetan youth while advancing broader frameworks for educational equity, human rights protection, and cultural preservation.

New Technologies and Transitional Justice

UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights

Justice Systems Technology Governance Human Rights
2025
Published

Overview

This brief examines the role of artificial intelligence and digital technologies in transitional justice processes, including fact-finding missions, accountability mechanisms, and documentation of human rights violations. It provides a comprehensive analysis of opportunities for enhanced efficiency and reliability, while critically addressing emerging risks including algorithmic bias, over-reliance on automated systems, and responsibility gaps in technology governance.

Key Recommendations

  • → Establish mandatory provenance standards and data transparency requirements for technology providers in justice contexts
  • → Implement human-rights due diligence frameworks assessing technological systems before deployment in sensitive sectors
  • → Create clear accountability mechanisms for errors, bias, and unintended consequences of AI-assisted justice processes
  • → Maintain human oversight and decision-making authority in all justice-related determinations

Authors: Nyida Gyal

Youth-Led Educational Interventions for Displaced Youth

European Commission, DG Employment, Social Affairs & Inclusion

Education Equity Refugee Youth Youth Leadership
2026
Under Review

Overview

This comprehensive report focuses on refugee and minority youth facing educational marginalization. It examines material resource gaps, the efficacy of youth-led governance models, and digital technology's role in educational access and equity. The brief draws from Tibetan diaspora experiences and global examples to propose frameworks that center youth agency while addressing systemic barriers to educational parity.

Key Recommendations

  • → Adopt needs-based funding models ensuring equitable resource allocation to refugee and minority students
  • → Establish youth-led governance structures in educational programs, creating meaningful decision-making roles for students
  • → Develop rights-safeguarding digital identity systems protecting student privacy and enabling seamless access across institutions
  • → Guarantee material and digital learning parity through mandatory accessibility standards and assistive technology provision
Under Review
Authors: Nyida Gyal, Thomas Millward, Khadotso Gyal, Tyler Jeong

Rights of Persons Belonging to National, Ethnic, Religious and Linguistic Minorities

UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights

Minority Rights Diaspora Communities Political Participation
2026
Published

Overview

Drawing from Tibetan diaspora experiences and global minority community perspectives, this brief addresses systemic challenges faced by stateless and diaspora populations. It examines barriers to political participation, identity documentation, digital governance access, and cultural representation. The submission provides actionable recommendations for international policy frameworks recognizing diaspora rights and supporting minority self-determination.

Key Recommendations

  • → Adopt inclusive documentation practices recognizing diverse forms of identity and belonging
  • → Ensure meaningful political participation pathways for diaspora communities in policy decisions affecting their constituencies
  • → Develop accessible digital tools and platforms for diaspora civic engagement and community governance
  • → Provide international legal protections for diaspora minority self-governance initiatives
SSRN
Authors: Khadotso Gyal, Thomas Millward, Nyida Gyal

Impact of Mental Health Challenges on Young People's Human Rights

UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights

Mental Health Youth Rights Psychosocial Support
2026
Under Review

Overview

This brief reframes youth mental health as a fundamental human rights issue, examining how mental health challenges intersect with and impair rights to health, education, non-discrimination, participation, and privacy. It provides evidence of disproportionate impacts on marginalized youth and offers policy recommendations for rights-based mental health approaches integrated with education, justice, and social protection systems.

Key Recommendations

  • → Enact rights-based mental health legislation centering youth agency and consent in service provision
  • → Integrate youth-inclusive mental health assessments in education and social policy development
  • → Establish stronger governance frameworks for digital mental health tools, protecting data privacy and preventing misuse
  • → Ensure universal access to quality mental health services with particular attention to marginalized and displaced populations
Under Review
Authors: Khadotso Gyal, Nyida Gyal
