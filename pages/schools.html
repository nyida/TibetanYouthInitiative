Partner Schools

Educational institutions in Dharamsala and beyond where we collaborate to support Tibetan youth.

Our partnerships are built on shared commitment to educational equity, cultural preservation, and youth empowerment. We work collaboratively with school leadership, teachers, and families to identify needs and deliver targeted support aligned with institutional missions.

Tibetan Children's Village (TCV)

Established 1960 Dharamsala & India-wide

Overview

Tibetan Children's Village is the largest network of educational institutions serving Tibetan refugee communities. Established to care for Tibetan orphans and destitute children, TCV has evolved into a comprehensive system spanning multiple residential villages, specialized schools, and day institutions across India.

Mission & Scope

TCV serves nearly 10,000 children and young adults across its network. The institution is dedicated to providing holistic education, cultural identity development, and preparation for self-reliant community participation. Each child receives care, education, vocational training, and cultural grounding in Tibetan language and values.

Our Partnership

  • ✓ Provide essential educational supplies to primary and secondary classes
  • ✓ Support cultural programs and Tibetan language instruction initiatives
  • ✓ Coordinate advocacy efforts amplifying TCV student voices in policy discussions
  • ✓ Engage with TCV youth in leadership and policy research collaborations

Mewoen Tsuglag Petoen School

Established 2005 Dharamsala

Overview

Mewoen Tsuglag Petoen School represents a model approach to Tibetan education in diaspora, combining contemporary pedagogical methods with traditional Tibetan language and cultural studies. Founded in 2005, the school serves as a model institution for preserving cultural identity while meeting international educational standards.

Educational Approach

Managed by the Sambhota Tibetan Schools Society, MTP School implements Tibetan-medium instruction through primary grades (Class I-III), introducing English and other languages in higher classes. This linguistic approach ensures students develop strong Tibetan literacy while acquiring global communication skills. The curriculum integrates Tibetan history, values, and cultural traditions throughout subjects.

Our Partnership

  • ✓ Support cultural curriculum implementation and Tibetan studies programs
  • ✓ Facilitate student participation in policy research and advocacy initiatives
  • ✓ Collaborate on cultural preservation and heritage transmission strategies

Yongling Crèche & Kindergarten

Established 1984 McLeod Ganj

Overview

Yongling Crèche & Kindergarten was established to meet the educational and childcare needs of the exile Tibetan community in McLeod Ganj. For over four decades, the institution has provided early childhood education in a nurturing, culturally grounded environment supporting working families and contributing to the Tibetan government-in-exile's educational mandate.

Mission & Services

The school serves approximately 75 children across crèche and kindergarten levels. Yongling provides:

  • • Early childhood education in supportive, play-based learning environments
  • • Hostel facilities for students requiring residential support
  • • Foundation in Tibetan language and cultural values during formative years

Our Partnership

  • ✓ Provide age-appropriate learning materials and developmental resources
  • ✓ Support creative and artistic programming for early childhood development
  • ✓ Facilitate family engagement and community programs
  • ✓ Advocate for early childhood education equity in policy discussions

Our partners are dedicated to preserving Tibetan language, heritage, and values. Our support strengthens these core institutional missions.

Accountability

Partnership relationships ensure transparency, ongoing collaboration, and measurable outcomes benefiting students and communities.
